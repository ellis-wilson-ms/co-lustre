--- Comment (adilger, Mon, 2 Mar 2026 22:25:53 +0000) ---
jinshanx  I was looking at your old patch https://review.whamcloud.com/8656 ("LU-4198 clio: generalize cl_sync_io") while trying to restore the ability to interrupt a stuck IO.

Your old patch is removing the call to `cl_io_cancel()` (which was the only caller of this function, and was eventually removed in later patches), and it appears that the code now just waits forever until the IO is finished (which may never happen) and can never be interrupted, rather than trying to abort the in-flight IO and then exit quickly with an error.  There were some later changes to the waiting functions, but I think 8565 is the patch that fundamentally broke being able to interrupt an IO in cl_sync_io_wait().

Changing the (existing) `wait_event_idle()` to `l_wait_event_abortable()` allows the wait to be interrupted, but it immediately crashes in several sanityn test cases like:
