I think it should be enough to check their lnet status and don't do mount attempt until it is 'discovered'

That would help the test case to pass, but doesn't really help users trying to mount the filesystem in real life. Should the retry interval be shorter until some real LNet interface is discovered and connected?  Before that point, it isn't worthwhile to try and send an RPC, but looping locally on LNet interface discovery doesn't put any load on the server (unlike resending MGS_CONNECT RPCs in a loop from thousands of clients would).
