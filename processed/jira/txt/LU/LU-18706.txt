--- Description ---
Mounting a Lustre target in read-only can be useful in case of disaster recovery. For example, when the underlying device is compromised. The Gael's presentation "Lustre on clown drives" from the LAD 2023 illustrates this kind of use cases.

Reproducer:

[root@dev ~]# ~eaujames/lustre-release/lustre/tests/llmount.sh
...
[root@dev ~]# lctl lustre_build_version 
Lustre version: 2.16.51_103_gb4748cb
[root@dev lustre]# cd /mnt/lustre
[root@dev lustre]# printf "%s\n" testfile{001..500} | xargs -I{} -P20 dd if=/dev/zero of={} count=1 bs=1M
...
[root@dev lustre]# mount -tlustre
