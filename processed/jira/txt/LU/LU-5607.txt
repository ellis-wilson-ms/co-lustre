--- Comment (adilger, Fri, 12 Sep 2014 17:32:02 +0000) ---
It is a bad idea that Lustre depends directly on struct posix_acl_xattr_entry (a kernel data structure) for the wire protocol.  It would be better to define our own copy of struct lustre_acl_xattr_entry in lustre_idl.h and "convert" to/from this structure for the network RPCs.  We could LASSERT() that they are the same size in the packing routines to ensure we catch any potential changes to the structure.

There is not a very big risk of this structure changing in the kernel, since it is also stored directly to disk for ext2/3/4, but there may be risk of change if/when the NFSv4-style ACLs are introduced at some point.
