--- Comment (adilger, Wed, 22 Oct 2025 18:00:30 +0000) ---
simmonsja, could you please look into this?  It is a regular cause of IPv6 test session failures, and is preventing the review-dne-ipv6 test sessions from being enforced. 

IPv6 can vary in size up to 42 bytes in total

Colin and I were discussing this, and if we remove the : from the IPv6 string and fully printed all quads of the address (no abbreviation, just a full hex string), then the address would only be 32 bytes, the same as a UUID, and will always fit. This has the benefit that the NID of the server would still be visible in the device name.
