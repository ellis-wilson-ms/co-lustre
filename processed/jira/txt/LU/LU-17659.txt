--- Description ---
If quota was earlier enabled with lctl conf_param lustre.quota.ost|mdt, it won't be totally disabled with "lctl set_param -P osd*.*.quota_slave.enabled=none".

The problem is that parameters from "conf_param" are handled before parameters set with lctl set_param -P. Thus if quota is enabled by conf_param it could start quota_reintegration process despite the fact quota_slave.enabled is none. Though quota will be disabled in the end after applying "lctl set_param" parameters, started reintegration process may cause problems on systems that require to totally disable quota to avoid kernel panics caused by problems in quota code.

To reproduce the problem:

lctl conf_param lustre.quota.ost=u
lctl set_param -P osd*.*.quota_slave.enable=none
umount /mnt/lustre-ost1
lctl dk > /dev/null
mount -t lustre -o loop /tmp/lustre-ost1 /mnt/lustre-ost1
lctl dk > logs
grep qsd logs
00040000:00020000:5.0:1710839020.455563:0:23985:0:(qsd_reint.c:635:qqi_reint_delayed()) lustre-MDT0000: Delaying reintegration for qtype:0 until pending updates are flushed.
00040000:00020000:5.0:1710839020.458242:0:23985:0:(qsd_reint.c:635:qqi_reint_delayed()) lustre-OST0001: Delaying reintegration for qtype:0 until pending updates are flushed.
