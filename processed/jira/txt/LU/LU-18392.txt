--- Comment (dongyang, Fri, 1 Nov 2024 04:53:13 +0000) ---
OK, I think now I figured out what happened.

The new test case recovery_small/160 checks for destroys_in_flight, and it should be a low number to indicate the osp destroy on MDT doesn't get blocked by group locks.

The EIO looks scary on the OST/ofd, but that is not from ldiskfs, it was returned by ofd_destroy_by_fid() when the object is under group lock.

So when osp gets the EIO reply, the destroy_in_flight got reduced and the object is put on error_list to retry later after a timeout.

Now sometimes when osp retries the destroy, the rpc is actually done on ofd because the threads holding group lock is done, but it's not yet committed. On osp we got the reply with transid, but the commit callback still needs sometime to be triggered to reduce destroys_in_flight, when the test case checks te destoys_in_flight it will be high, as the rpcs are still pending, but eventually it will finish.

I think it's more like a test case issue, working on fixing the test case.
