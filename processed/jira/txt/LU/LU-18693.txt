--- Comment (colmstea, Fri, 30 Jan 2026 19:13:41 +0000) ---
I investigated our NFS servers and discovered the ARP table limits had the default GC values which are far too low for the amount of traffic it handles:

Default limit: 1,024 ARP entries

Actual connections: 1,391 (onyx-204), 1,436 (trevis-204)

Impact

	ARP table at 100% capacity (1,022 / 1,024 entries)

	Kernel performing 2,788 forced garbage collections per second

	Hundreds of NFSv4 "testing state ID with incorrect client ID" errors per second

	Connection instability and performance degradation

I increased the limits yesterday and the ARP entries balooned up to 1,700, all of the GCs stopped and the "testing state ID with incorrect client ID" errors went away. This was likely the cause of this ticket. I'm closing it. Please re-open with any new occurrences.
