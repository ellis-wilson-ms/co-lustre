--- Description ---
Leaving a couple of words here to don't loose what have been discussed with hongchao.zhang.

Taking into account upcoming metadata redundancy changes, we could start thinking of how to distribute the load at the QMT. Since all MDTs would have synchronised quota limits, it is possible to divide all possible quota IDs for several ranges and attach each range to be serviced by QMT at a separate  MDT. I.e. range1[0-99999] could be serviced by the QMT at the MDT0, [10000-19999] by the QMT at the MDT1 etc. In case if MDT0 is offline or removed, MDT1 may start to support the 1st range[0-99999] - it already has quota limits for that and the only thing needs to be done is granted space recalculation for the IDs from this range, i.e. quota reintegration process should be started at the quota slaves.

It's just a raw idea without a deep investigation. Probably some smarter mechanism of IDs distribution should be considered instead of simple ID ranges dividing. The 1st obvious drawback of suggested approach is how support LQAs if ID ranges are divided between the MDTs, probably there should be an exception for the LQA. I believe other potential problems could be found, but putting this idea here for the future if we would need to improve the load at the QMT.
