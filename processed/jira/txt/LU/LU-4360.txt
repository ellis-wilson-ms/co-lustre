--- Comment (green, Sat, 28 Dec 2013 01:58:37 +0000) ---
This bug was introduced by Isaac in commit e2a2fab993d01597010cb2b44df44a522af0eec8

there is a very profilic use after free in ksocknal_send - once it calls ksocknal_launch_packet - this might trigger a callback that frees the request, and then ksocknal_send tries to access the request again with 

        if (lntmsg->msg_vmflush)

                cfs_memory_pressure_restore(mpflag);

and we die

Potentially other LNDs are succeptible to a similar problem as well.

I verified that moving call to ksocknal_launch_packet after the vmflush check fixes the issue for me, but I am not 100% sure if it actually never allocates anything (making it the valid fix) or not.
