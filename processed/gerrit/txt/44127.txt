Sebastien, does this preserve the broken ACL on the target file, or is it discarded?  On the one hand, we don't want to prevent files from being copied into Lustre (not all external xattrs are relevant, and ACLs on non-Linux filesystems may be formatted differently), but we also don't want to store those xattrs in Lustre if they are malformed.

Is this just a case of setxattr trying to store a zero-byte system.posix_acl_default xattr to the file?

It would be good to have a test case for this.

--- Message (Sebastien Buisson, 2021-07-06 12:50:00.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1:

The purpose of this patch is simply to make sure Lustre behaves similarly with or without nodemap enabled. This is why nodemap_map_acl() must return 'size' even in case of malformed ACL: like without nodemap, it will be processed later. More precisely, in mdd_xattr_set() the xattr value is converted to an acl, and if it is not valid it is deleted.

I will add a test in sanity.sh.
