--- Message (Andreas Dilger, 2020-06-21 19:47:38.000000000) ---
Patch Set 3:

Arshad, at a minimum the ZFS fallocate call will verify that there is currently enough space in the filesystem (and also on the specific OSTs allocated for that file) to hold a full copy of the file. Even if that space is not strictly reserved under ZFS it is better than writing 100GB of data for a file and then running out of space before the copy is finished.

One minor concern is that with preallocated files on ldiskfs they are saved as unwritten extents, which need to be converted to normal extents before use, which causes some overhead. As a result, it makes sense to use larger O_DIRECT buffers when copying the file, probably 16MB or 32MB. That will also improve performance during the copy.
