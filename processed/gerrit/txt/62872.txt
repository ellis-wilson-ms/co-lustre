LU-13296 llite: stop caching kstatfs to fix panic by zero div

The project statfs cache previously stored a full kstatfs
(psc_sfs) and returned it from the fast-path without
taking the per-project mutex. Because psc_age and psc_sfs
were updated separately, a window existed where psc_age
was refreshed while psc_sfs remained partially or entirely
uninitialized. Concurrent statfs() callers could observe
this stale or uninitialized kstatfs, including f_bsize == 0.

Since quota calculations divide by f_bsize, a zero value
caused invalid statfs output and could trigger a kernel
panic due to divide-by-zero.

The root problem is that kstatfs and f_bsize must not
be cached: f_bsize belongs to the caller's statfs
context, and a pre-computed kstatfs cannot be safely
reused across callers.

Fix this by removing psc_sfs entirely. Cache only
invariant quota metadata (block limits, inode limits,
current usage), and apply these limits to the
caller-supplied kstatfs via a new helper. This
guarantees that f_bsize always comes from the current
statfs call and is never stale or uninitialized.

This eliminates the race, prevents f_bsize corruption,
and removes the possibility of quota rounding errors
or divide-by-zero panics.
