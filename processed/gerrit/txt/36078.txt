LU-12631 llite: report latency for filesystem ops

Add the elapsed time of VFS operations to the llite stats
counter, instead of just tracking the number of operations,
to allow tracking of operation round-trip latency.

Update sanity test_127[ab] to check that llite.*.stats and
osc.*.stats counter shows read/write stats in usec, and
fix code style nearby.

> Can you explain how the tests check the latency part of the stats?
 > Unless I missed something, it doesn't seem like that's specifically queried?

I couldn't think of any specific way to validate the latency numbers beyond just visually inspecting the output. It isn't possible to easily compare them even to the OSC stats, because the llite stats include cached results (ie. what is visible to applications), in addition to requests that need RPCs to complete.

--- Message (Patrick Farrell, 2019-09-16 18:24:24.000000000) ---
Patch Set 8:

"I couldn't think of any specific way to validate the latency numbers beyond just visually inspecting the output. It isn't possible to easily compare them even to the OSC stats, because the llite stats include cached results (ie. what is visible to applications), in addition to requests that need RPCs to complete."

Sure, it makes sense that we can't do a ton of comparing them and obviously verifying the reported timing through, eg, external measurement of time for an op is going to be so racey as to be basically useless.

That said, can we at least validate that the stats are generated and output, ie, something like "usec appears in the output in the expected place" after I do [some operation]?
