LU-12668 lov: batch parity reads per-stripe for EC

Refactor lov_ec_read_parity_pages into
lov_ec_read_parity_stripe which reads all pages for
one parity stripe in a single cl_io_submit_sync call.
Hoist parity reads and lov_ec_recover_init out of the
per-page reconstruction loop -- pre-fetch all needed
parity pages before reconstruction, then kmap during
the per-page loop. Delete unused
lov_ec_read_data_pages (superseded by committed data
batching).

Patch Set 28:

PS28: amended ec_start_read_fault in the introducing commit (64079 LU-12668 tests: add EC recovery tests) to replace drop_caches with cancel_lru_locks osc.

The prior ec_start_read_fault did:
  $LCTL set_param fail_loc=0x8000020f fail_val=0
  echo 3 > /proc/sys/vm/drop_caches

Problem: pages from a buffered write stay bulk-pinned for RPC replay until the OST commits, and the kernel mapping_evict_folio refuses to evict folios whose refcount includes that pin. drop_caches silently evicts nothing for those pages. The 57 tests that call ec_start_read_fault after a buffered dd were therefore reading from cache on the second md5sum rather than exercising the EC recovery path -- the fail_loc never fired.

cancel_lru_locks osc cancels the DLM extent locks, which waits for in-flight IO to complete and then actively discards the associated pages. This works whether or not bulk pins are held.

No test bodies changed; the raw "echo 3 > /proc/sys/vm/drop_caches" lines that follow ec_start_read_fault in some tests are now redundant but harmless.
