(4 comments)

I am wondering if the ext4-filename-encode patch, at least as it is inspired from rhel8, is actually required on rhel9.

Re-reading LU-16369, the ext4-filename-encode patch was introduced in rhel8 because on this kernel (4.18), ldiskfs is not encryption aware. So when we mounted an MDT as ldiskfs, we were getting -EPERM when accessing encrypted sub-directories. Now, rhel9 being based on a 5.14 kernel, we should not face the same problem when browsing an MDT mounted as ldiskfs that contains Lustre encrypted files. Have you tried that without the ext4-filename-encode patch?
