Brian,
I'm not wholly against the use of "redhat-fc18" instead of tracking the specific "3.6-fc18" or "3.7-fc18" or whatever the latest kernel is.  The other alternative is to use "3.x-fc18" or similar so that it doesn't have to be bumped for every kernel version.

The difference with older 2.6-fc15 and such is that the "2.6" part was constant for all of the releases and the ".18" or ".32" part was incremented, but excluded from our naming convention.
