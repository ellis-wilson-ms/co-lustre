AFAIR, one of the reasons the initial T10 patch was abandoned was the requirement from Intel to update the design and network protocols so that different checksumming models can be used, not just T10, but also Merkle tree based, and not use separate connection flags for each checksumming type. However, this patch sticks to a single protocol and single connection flag. Has anything changed since then? Wouldn't it be easier just to rework the original patch than duplicating that work ? I cannot remember any terrible defects in it that require rewriting everything from scratch. Lustre itself does not benefit from splitting a single feature into phases either - it just makes coding easier.

Andrew, the other reason the previous T10-PI patch was unpleasant was that it sent all of the T10-PI GRD tags over the network (it might have also sent the APP and REF tags, I don't recall).  Even with only the GRD tags, it would be 2 bytes/sector, or 4KB of extra data for a 1MB RPC.  For a 16MB RPC it would be 64KB of extra data, which would be very inefficient.

One benefit of the new patch is the checksum-of-checksums approach, which reduces the 4KB (or 64KB) to a 32-bit checksum that can easily fit into the existing Lustre RPC checksum field.
