I'm not sure what this means?  Normally, we use the "self" ticket to mean that the patch is broken and failed due to a problem it introduced itself.  Retesting a patch that is broken until it passes is not a great way to get stable code.

If the testing problem is *not* related to this patch, then using the "self" patch as the reason for retesting is a cop-out.  It is already very easy to [Associate] a ticket with a test failure, then [Retest] the session.  At least take the time to understand why a patch failed testing, so that we can focus on the tests that are failing most often.

I agree it is unlikely that the configure changes introduced test failures, but stranger things have happened.  Also, investigating, and possibly fixing the intermittent test failures makes life better for everyone.
