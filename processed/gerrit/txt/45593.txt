LU-15245 mdc: GET(X)ATTR to READPAGE portal

Send the MDS_GETATTR and MDS_GETXATTR RPCs to the
MDS_READPAGE_PORTAL instead of the default portal to avoid
deadlocks with other MDS_REINT RPCs that may block all of
the MDS service threads on that portal.

This deadlock occurs with MDS_GETXATTR when selinux is
enabled, because getxattr becomes part of lookup, so it
takes a reference on a lock used for lookup.  However, all
of the MDS service threads on the default portal can be
consumed by threads waiting for that lock, resulting in
a deadlock when the getxattr can't be processed.

--- Message (Andreas Dilger, 2022-01-05 21:01:49.000000000) ---
Patch Set 6:

> I'm wondering what is the reasons why a RPC should go to a portal
 > instead of another one?

Just FYI, due to the RPC handling implementation, all of the MDS portals can potentially handle all MDS_* RPC requests, and all OSS portals can handle all OST_* RPC requests.

The main reason to have separate portals is to avoid deadlocks when the completion of one RPC may depend on a second RPC to be processed.  This is normally only a problem when the number of in-flight RPCs is larger than the maximum MDS thread count (e.g. large numbers of clients/threads performing the same RPC operations at one time).

The other reason for separate portals is to avoid high latency for "fast" RPCs that might otherwise be delayed significantly in the RPC queue (e.g. OST stat while many large writes are being handled by a slow disk).  These wouldn't necessarily deadlock like the first case, but would introduce potentially unacceptable latency for interactive users (though they still may contend on internal filesystem locking and block device IO queues).
