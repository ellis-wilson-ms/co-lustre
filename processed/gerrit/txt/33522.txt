LU-11359 mdt: fix mdt_dom_discard_data() timeouts

The mdt_dom_discard_data() issue new lock to cause lock
discard for all conflicting client locks. This was done in
context of unlink RPC processing and may cause it to stuck
waiting for client to cancel their locks though it is not
needed.
Patch sends discard ASTs to blocking locks directly without
waiting the client responses.

I've missed Oleg's questions in reply, it is important to answer them because that explains the problem I am trying to solve.
First of all, unlink on MDT is not the same as on OST in two moments:
1) it happens immediately for for metadata and data, while on OST objects are deleted with significant delay - through OSP llog. Moreover, nobody cares how long it will take to destroy OST objects
2) on OST object is always destroyed when destroy RPC arrived because has no open counts and hardlinks. On MDT we know that object is going to be deleted only at the end of unlink.
The 2) fact answers question why we cannot take lock with discard flag first, before unlink itself but can do that on OFD, we just don't know that object will be destroyed, so first we have to finish unlink then check if object gone and ask for data discard. Also we cannot even take DOM bit in first lock for unlink operation - because that would cause data flush though it can be not needed.
That is why discard is done after unlink is finished and we know for sure that object is unlinked. For open-unlink case discard is called from CLOSE path, when last open counter is dropped.

Now about problem with discard itself. As I said in 1), on OFD that process is hidden and nobody cares how long it will take, there are just no other activity on object. On MDT it is not so, consider unlink case, we are inside UNLINK RPC processing from client, now we are taking lock to discard data, and will wait until lock is taken, so in case of any IO activity on client we will wait until it is finished and lock is canceled. All that time the server will not reply on UNLINK RPC to a client. That causes timeouts in racer quite often because it looks like unlink is just stuck. In fact the taking of discard lock is working as barrier for all ongoing IO and cache flush/discard on clients, though it is not needed at least because that discard lock is just an optimization to avoid useless data flush for deleted files.
To avoid such waiting on MDT, I've decided to issue BL ASTs with DISCARD flag directly for all blocking lock, without waiting for an answer, so it is just notification to clients to discard their data for that resource.

Since MDT unlink shouldn't guarantee there are no outstanding concurrent IO remains we might see that racy IO, (e.g. occasional flush before discard happens or just ongoing IO) may find object is gone. That situation exists with OST objects as well but is very rare due to OST objects remains for some time after MDT unlink.
