LU-18489 readahead: use page index when increase stride ra window

Currently when we increase the stride readahead window,
we shift ras->ras_rpc_pages to bytes and pass it to
ras_stride_increase_window() to work out the new window size,
then the result is shifted back to pages, saved to
ras->ras_window_pages.

Let's keep everything in units of pages when calculating
the new window, without shifting to/from bytes. It is in pages
to begin with, and IO will be issued in units of pages.

This also eliminates a bug where currently the increased window
is calculated as
((ras_rpc_pages << PAGE_SHIFT) / stride_byte) * stride_length,
for a small stride_byte less than a page size this could end up
with a huge unintended readahead window, creating many small 4K reads.

For a same work load which reads 4 bytes and then seek 180k repeatly,
this patch reduces the runtime from 20 mins to 6 mins.

Without patch
llite.data00-ffff8bcb1b8d2800.read_ahead_stats=
readahead_pages           1845 samples [pages] 1 524288 19747507

With patch
llite.data00-ffff9d396ecb8800.read_ahead_stats=
readahead_pages           4 samples [pages] 122 643 1410

No performance degradations are observed on
IO500 ior-easy and ior-hard (all buffered IO)
vClient 8 CPU cores, 30GB RAM, 100Gbps IB

1 client

Hopefully Patrick will recall, but the stride needs to be calculated in bytes, otherwise it can become unaligned for cases where it is not an even multiple of the PAGE_SIZE.

If the patch is just converting the byte-granular stride calculation to pages for submitting readahead, then that is OK, but if it is reverting the stride calculation to be page-granular then that will be a regression.
