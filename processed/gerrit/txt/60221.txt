LU-19168 mdd: fix spinlock bad magic during OBD_FAIL_MDS_FS_SETUP

Tests 5h and 26 in conf-sanity.sh that use fail_loc 0x135
(OBD_FAIL_MDS_FS_SETUP) were hitting "spinlock bad magic" errors
during cleanup.

The issue occurs when the failure injection triggers during MDT
initialization in mdt_init0() before mdd_changelog_init() is called.
During error cleanup, mdt_stack_fini() calls mdd_changelog_fini()
which tries to access uninitialized spinlocks (mc_lock and
mc_user_lock), causing the kernel to detect corrupted spinlock
magic numbers.

Fix by moving the spinlock initialization from mdd_changelog_init()
to mdd_init0(), ensuring the spinlocks are always initialized early
in the MDD device setup process, even if mdd_changelog_init() is
never called due to early failures.

The execution order is:
1. mdt_init0() -> mdt_stack_init() -> mdd_device_alloc() -> mdd_init0()
   (spinlocks now initialized here)
2. OBD_FAIL_MDS_FS_SETUP failure injection triggers
3. mdt_stack_fini() -> mdd_changelog_fini() (spinlocks safe to use)
