> I don't like the fact that you drop and reacquire the lnet_res_lock() if you need to allocate memory.  It isn't at all clear to me why that is safe.  What is the lock protecting here anyway?
I think I biggish comment would be needed to justify that.

The res_lock is protecting us against concurrent access to the md_rspt_ptr field of the MD. In the case where there is already a response tracker attached to the MD, that response tracker may be on one of the monitor thread's response tracker queues. It can lookup the MD from the handle stored in the rspt.

I'm really glad you asked about this, because I didn't realize that I was relying on a refcount on the MD that isn't there anymore. Previously this function was called after the MD had been attached to the lnet message. The attachment adds a refcount to the MD that I had been relying on to make the dropping of the res lock safe. Whoops...
