Patch Set 1: Code-Review-1

I'm not really on board with this approach.

I think instead we should take a step back and consider lustre_idl.h's purpose.  As I understand it, the purpose is to define wire protocol.

DRANGE does not define wire protocol.  It is just a convenience macro for parsing FIDs.

ostid_set_id does not define wire protocol.  It just helps access structures that may be part of the wire protocol.

The list goes on.  Intel has let a lot of things into lustre_idl.h in recent history that are not wire protocol.  You folks haven't been following the rules set out in lustre_idl.h's comments:

"All structs being declared here should be built from simple fixed-size types (__u8, __u16, __u32, __u64) or be built from other types or structs also declared in this file."

The face that libcfs.h and lustre_errno.h now need to be included are evidence of violations of the rule.

The solution is not to ifdef out the things that user space shouldn't have seen in the first place.  The proper solution is to move those things out of lustre_idl.h that did not belong there in the first place.
