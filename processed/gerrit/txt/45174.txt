Patch Set 3:

> From looking at the discussion in the ticket, do I understand
 > correctly that we only want to apply the NO_RETRY flag when
 > allocating router buffers?

That was the concern raised in the ticket, because the number of router buffers can be specified directly by userspace, and the result is the machine going OOM rather than returning a sensible error, or (IMHO preferably) just working within the limits of available RAM.

That said, there are probably other places where this may be useful, but they can be fixed in a separate patch.
