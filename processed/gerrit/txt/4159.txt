(1 inline comment)

James, I'm not against removing dead code, but I'm also not a huge fan of increasing the scope of a patch beyond its original goal.

The goal of this patch was to disable the building of obdfilter when ldiskfs is disabled.  The danger of making a patch larger and larger is that it increases the "objection and failure surface" of the patch.  For example, there _might_ be code in CMM that is needed for the DNE project, which could block this patch from landing, or causes some corner testing case to fail.

Due to the increased scope, we may need more inspections on this patch to ensure that it is OK to land.

Hence, smaller, focused patches are easier to understand, inspect, test, and land.  Removal of dead code could also be done in a follow-on patch that has a more clear commit comment describing what was done.

I'm not going to reject this patch as it stands now, because I think it is OK, and there isn't a reason to delay it, but just some things to keep in mind for the future.
