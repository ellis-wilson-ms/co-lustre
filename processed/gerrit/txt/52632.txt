LU-17186 utils: replace gethostby*() with get*info()

This patch replaces the deprecated gethostbyname() and
gethostbyaddr() functions with getaddrinfo() and getnameinfo()
functions respectively.

The getaddrinfo() function combines the functionality provided by the
gethostbyname() and getservbyname() functions into a single interface,
but unlike the latter functions, getaddrinfo() is reentrant and allows
programs to eliminate IPv4-versus-IPv6 dependencies.

The getnameinfo() function is the inverse of getaddrinfo(): it
converts a socket address to a corresponding host and service, in a
protocol-independent manner. It combines the functionality of
gethostbyaddr() and getservbyport(), but unlike those functions,
getnameinfo() is reentrant and allows programs to eliminate
IPv4-versus-IPv6 dependencies.

I think there is lots of room for improvement in libcfs_ip_str2addr()

I just gave this patch a try, and it seems it breaks nid-to-host resolution.

For instance, *without this patch*, when activating Kerberos authentication, the lgss_keyring command called by request-key says:

   Nov 13 15:01:55 lnode-vm6.makalu lgss_keyring[224233]: [224233]:INFO:ipv4_nid2hostname(): SOCKLND: net 0x20000, addr 0xce38a8c0 => lnode-vm6

On the same system, *with this patch applied*, it says:

   Nov 13 14:44:55 lnode-vm6.makalu lgss_keyring[207702]: [207702]:INFO:ipv4_nid2hostname(): SOCKLND: net 0x20000, addr 0xc0a838ce => 192.168.56.206

So it is not capable of returning the hostname. And the addr seems to be 'flipped': 0xce38a8c0 without the patch vs. 0xce38a8c0 with this patch.

Kerberos is just an example where nid-to-host resolution functions touched by this patch are used (and convenient for me to exercise), but I guess there are other places, which might explain other failures seen in Maloo test results.
