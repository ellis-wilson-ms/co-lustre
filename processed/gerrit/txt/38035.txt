Patch Set 2: Code-Review+1

Maybe I'm misunderstanding, but this isn't quite what I was thinking.  This provides an interface to retrieve multiple access logs at once, which is good in itself, but I don't think it is enough to reduce the torrent of records that will hit the central coordinator.

I was more thinking that the log reader would do some aggregation of records locally on each OST (e.g. merge records for each object) and send only aggregate IOPS/sizes to the coordinator.  That helps the coordinator to scale with increasing numbers of OSTs, and reduces the number of operations it has to do itself.  If necessary, the load of processing the records is on the OSS where the records are being generated and will throttle the record generation to some extent.

Maybe this is just the first stage in that pipeline, to improve the record processing for the log reader, and aggregation is the next step?
