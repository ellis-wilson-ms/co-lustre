--- Message (Neil Brown, 2020-07-21 04:16:41.000000000) ---
Patch Set 6:

> Neil any insights to this behavior.

Are you using splice() to attach a pipe to a lustre file open for read, then reading data from the pipe?
When you say "the number of buffers is reported to be 16" is that the number of pipe_buffers in a 'struct pipe_inode_info'? - the ring_size?

Presumably when you are reading into the pipe, you expect all the buffers to be empty and the pages NULL, and they get filled by the read.
If the filesystem stores pages in the pagecache, then the pages don't need to be allocated - the pipe_buffer points directly to the page-cache page.
If the filesystem doesn't store pages in the pagecache - e.g. NFS doing O_DIRECT reads - then read_iter() method needs to call iov_iter_get_pages_alloc() at some point to allocate them - then the content gets copied in.
