--- Message (Patrick Farrell, 2019-06-04 17:02:14.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1:

I'm kind of bothered by the use of rq_no_resend in general.  I believe it was originally (and is still *mostly*) used for 'optional' requests, like async statfs, where failure is not fatal.  Presumably that's why Alex put this check here.

But it's also been merged with no delay in a lot of use cases, seemingly just because they 'go together'?

For example, the quotactl RPC is not optional (it's an explicit command), and we should resend it if needed.

So if I'm correct about what no_resend is supposed to mean, I think we should try to clean that up.  If it really followed what looks like the intended usage, this check would be OK...

(Since cleaning that up may be difficult, I like Alex's suggestion about trying one reconnect, I am just voicing a general concern.)
