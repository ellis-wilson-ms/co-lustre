LU-398 ptlrpc: Add the NRS CRR-N policy

The CRR-N (Client-based Round Robin over NIDs) policy batch-schedules
all types of RPCs in a Round Robin manner according to the NID of the
client that generated the RPC; the maximum size of the batches is
configurable via interaction with lprocfs. The policy aims to provide
for better resource utilization across the cluster, and to help
shorten completion times of jobs in some cases by distributing
available bandwidth more evenly across client nodes.

--- Message (Andreas Dilger, 2013-01-22 22:52:26.000000000) ---
Patch Set 10:

Nikitas, per your comment about freeing the CRR instance information - I don't have a strong opinion either way.

I think it makes sense to free it when the refcount is dropped, since this would more closely match e.g. the ORR policy and probably others in the future, and would avoid pinning more memory than needed if there are many clients.  I don't think having a single alloc/free for the client per "burst" of IO is a problem at all (we do this on a per _request_ basis already).

Freeing the CRR instance when the refcount is dropped is _definitely_ preferable to the case where the CRR instance is kept around after the client is disconnected, or if the LRU adds overhead or lock contention on a single lock due to the need to keep the list in order.
