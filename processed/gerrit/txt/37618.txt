--- Commit Message ---
LU-00000 lnet: Convert spin locks to bit locks

Call chains originating from lnet_finalize force a must
not sleep due to spin locks.
Inspired by Neil's https://review.whamcloud.com/#/c/37068/

This is attempts a 'direct' translation from spin locks
to bit locks keeping the original sequence points but
acting more like lightweight mutexes that allow sleeping
while the lock(s) are held.

(6 comments)

Apart from the various specific issues I've noted below, I don't think this because I think it heads in entirely the wrong direction.
I don't think we should make lnet_res_lock() better, I think we should make it disappear.

I'd put a spinlock in 'struct lnet_res_container' and use that to protect rec_lh_hash.

It isn't entirely clear what else the lock protects, so analysing and documenting that would be a next step.  Then break it down to individual spinlocks and/or RCU usage.

These lnet_res_lock and lnet_net_lock locks are conceptually a lot like the BKL.  They were doubtlessly a simple expedient to get multicore code working, but they get used for two many different things and it becomes unclear what they really protect, so they become a maintenance problem.
Let's work to get rid of them.
