LU-18053 osc: add active osc_lru_shrink() limit

When discarding pages osc_lru_shrink() eventually calls
delete_from_page_cache() which must aquire a spin lock
on the page cache.  On systems with a large number of
CPUs this can result spin lock contention and soft
lockups.

In order to prevent this apply a per-filesystem limit
to the number of concurrent osc_lru_shrink() callers.
This relys on osc_lru_shrink() being called frequently
enough per OSC that under normal conditions there will
be a less contended opperunity to run.  When the force
flag is passed to osc_lru_shrink() it will block until
until under the maximum limit.  No attempt is made at
fairness.

A default limit of 4 was used to allow some concurrency
without risking overwhelming the page cache spin lock.

If the lock contention could be further reduced, for
example by implementing LU-12534 then it's likely the per
filesystem osc_lru_shrink() limit could be increased.
