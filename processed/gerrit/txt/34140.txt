I'm not against this, per-se, but don't feel the need to comb through the code making all of the ENTER/RETURN calls match.  That would be a lot of churn for relatively little value, and there are a hundred other things that you could be doing that have more value (man pages, improving "lfs find" functionality, bugs, features, etc).

This kind of stuff can (IMHO) also be cleaned up along with other changes (to some extent, as long as it doesn't swamp the original change).

I also don't mind having a standing ticket for trivial fixes like LU-930 or simple man pages, since I agree it is a bit of a hassle to open new tickets for every small patch, and a cleanup like this (it is a stretch to elevate it to the level of "fix") should be as low friction as possible and is not worthwhile to call out individually in the release notes, so tracking each one with an LU ticket on the release is IMHO overkill.
