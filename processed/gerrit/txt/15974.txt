Patch Set 3:

> I'm still not sure this is the correct approach. LFSCK prefetch the attributes from the targets and type of the target doesn't matter. when LFSCK finds a potential inconsistency, it acquires LDLM lock and re-fetch the attributes (IIRC). if you just disable those, then LFSCK's performance will suffer. let's discuss with Fan Yong first. probably we can invalidate the cache in osp_object_release() ?

Currently, only the cached OST-object's attr/xattr in OSP is valuable for LFSCK, because:

1) The cached MDT-object's attr/xattr maybe changed by other on remote MDT, that will cause the local OSP cache to be invalid, then may misguide the local LFSCK.

2) Compared with local MDT-object, remote MDT-object is relative rare case, cache remote MDT-object's attr/xattr in OSP will not bring significant performance benefit.
