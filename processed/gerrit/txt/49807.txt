--- Commit Message ---
LU-16483 ptlrpc: Track highest reply XID

Keep track of the highest XID that we've received a reply for.
When an OBD_PING expires, do not disconnect the import if the failed
XID is less than or equal to the last reply XID. This avoids situation
where a lost OBD_PING rpc causes a reconnect even though we've
completed other RPCs in the meantime.

--- Message (Andreas Dilger, 2023-01-28 00:24:30.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1: Code-Review+1

(2 comments)

I think this is OK, but the naming could probably be improved.

While "highest reply XID" isn't a perfect metric for identifying lost OBD_PING RPCs, since the XIDs are generated in increasing *creation* order, we at least know that an RPC that was (likely) sent before the OBD_PING has received a reply in the meantime.  It is still possible for the LNet sending of RPCs to be disordered by MR LND queues, and delayed unevenly by routers at least we know the peer is still alive, which is the goal of the pings.

While I'm happy to see this fix, what would be even better would be to investigate/implement the Gossip protocol at the LNet level to monitor peer health instead of using RPC timeouts to do this.
