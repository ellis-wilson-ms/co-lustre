Patch Set 12: (4 inline comments)

If Oleg is requesting the patch be updated again, I'd like to (politely :-) ask if the "in-between lines" are also converted to tabs.

Since this patch is changing so many lines in the ldlm files, it is a great opportunity to also do a lot of tab conversion.  That won't increase the "patch conflict surface area", but it will help convert a lot of lines to tabs that are otherwise kind of left ragged with alternating lines of tabs and spaces.  The policy is up to +/- 6 lines between two modified lines, or +/- 3 lines at the start/end of a function/structure, but you are free to use your own discretion to increase this if it finishes off a whole code block or function or structure.

I did this in another huge patch via "git format-patch HEAD~1" to get a patch file, and edited the patch directly (if you are comfortable with that sort of thing) in vim with a search for tabs highlighting where they are missing, then reset the branch to the previous commit via "git reset --hard HEAD~1", and re-imported the edited patch via "git am 0001-whatever-the-patch-is-called.patch".

If editing patches by hand isn't your cup of tea, just "git show > tt" highlighting the tabs shows where there are small clusters of in-between lines that are not using tabs, and then the actual file can be edited, and "git commit -a --amend" to merge the changes into the patch.
