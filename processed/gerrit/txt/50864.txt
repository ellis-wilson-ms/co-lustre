expect ccache files to be under /tmp/krb5cc_*.

The failure in sanity-krb5 test_151 is due to a missing Kerberos principal. In this test, the MGS is being kerberized: it gets configured to only accept connections from servers and clients if they use the correct Kerberos flavor. But this can work only if the MGS server has its own principal, in the form:

   lustre_mgs/<node>@<DOMAIN>

So for sanity-krb5 test_151 to pass in Maloo, the MGS specific principal must be declared in the KDC, and exported to keytab files, just like with other principals for MDS, OSS and client roles.
