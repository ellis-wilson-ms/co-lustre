Sorry my comment was confusing. What I meant is that you do

char *tmp = strchr(lustre_cfg_string(lcfg, 1), '=');

tmp is directly pointing to an offset in the lcfg buffer. So when you do *tmp = '\0' you are altering a lcfg buffer itself. That is not safe. A better that doesn't involve altering the lcfg buffer is to use snprintf %.*s and print only a specific length of the lcfg buffer which is what you are trying to do.
