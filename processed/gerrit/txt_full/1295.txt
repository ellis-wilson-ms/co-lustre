--- Commit Message ---
LU-617 temporary fix for the setattr from open

The setattr from open(open(O_TRUNC)) is serialized with
'cl_setattr_lock' on client and goes to a dedicate portal, which is
different with other reint operations, consequently, setattr RPC
can be parallel with other reint RPCs, and that result in the race of
updating last_transno/last_xid on server.

This temporary fix removes the LASSERT(req_is_replay(req)) in
mdt_last_rcvd_update().

--- Message (Andreas Dilger, 2011-08-26 17:49:12.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1: I would prefer that you didn't submit this

This change still makes me nervous to land right before the 2.1.0 release, because it hasn't gotten any serious testing during all of the previous 2.0.6x test cycles, yet it is changing some fundamental behaviour of Lustre that has existed since Lustre 0.5.20.4.  In my opinion it has a non-zero risk to cause a regression that won't be seen until running on a large cluster under load.

For 2.1.0 I'd prefer to remove LASSERT(req_is_replay(req)) from the MDS, and in the non-replay case only print the CERROR() and skip updating the last_rcvd file with the lower transno.  While this may not be strictly correct for a vanishingly small number of recovery cases, I think the lower risk of breaking 2.1.0 at this late stage outweighs the (IMHO) miniscule risk of a recovery bug.

At worst, I believe that the recovery bug may only cause a failed O_TRUNC setattr at open for MDS inodes with no objects that has a non-zero size (itself almost never used), and a crash after the higher transno has committed to disk but the setattr wasn't committed to disk.  At this point the recovery may fail due to the gap in transno, and the client would be evicted.

I think a more complete solution can be made after the 2.1.0 release, possibly going back to see if the client can avoid sending the O_TRUNC setattr entirely, since it is already sending the open RPC?  In any case, there will be a lot more freedom to experiment and test with low risk of breaking the release.

That said, if Oleg doesn't agree with this assessment, because he has a better understanding of this code than I do, then I am not _totally_ against it landing.
