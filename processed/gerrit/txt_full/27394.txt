LU-8324 hsm: prioritize HSM requests

This patch separates the coordinator's implementation from the way
it processes requests stored in its llog. This last part is now
handled by a scan policy. A scan policy implements a vector of 3
operations to init the policy's internal structure, process the
stored requests, and exit cleanly.

A new scan policy is defined: "priority_policy".
The working principles of the policy can be found in the comments of
"mdt_coordinator_priority_policy.c". Interactive HSM requests
(HSM_RESTORE and HSM_CANCEL) will basically be prioritized over other
types of requests (HSM_NOOP, HSM_ARCHIVE, and HSM_REMOVE).

Scan policies can be dynamically switched by writing the name of
the policy to use in the proc file "scan_policy" (which can be found
in the hsm proc directory).

This patch also adds tests in sanity-hsm (253 and 256).

Patch Set 2: Code-Review-1

Besides the build failure, there are some flaws in the algorithm used to batch requests according to their priority: (from most to least important)

- The traversal of spd->requests becomes too costful when cdt->max_requests is large and spd->requests is almost full;
- Non-prioritized requests cannot be replaced in spd->requests, even if they are beyond the barrier;
- prioritized requests fill the buffer from the "right side" whereas documentation shows they should fill the buffer from the left side (and vice-versa for non-priotized requests).
