LRU in general isn't a performance concern as long as it's vaguely reasonable (there are exceptions, but you get yourself most of the benefit for most cases with a simple reasonable-ish LRU).  The issue with SLV is that it has been persistently buggy, causing periodic issues where all locks are dropped and/or a particular client is told it can't have any locks.  Variations of these have occurred periodically since I started working on Lustre and they're still around, though rare.

So no performance concern with SLV.  For the regular LRU, I actually also don't really have a suggestion - I think the issue of frequently used locks getting pushed out is going to be hard to find a real workload for where the impact is measurable, since getting a new lock isn't too hard.  I actually think unless we can find a workload, improving it might not be the best use of time. :)
