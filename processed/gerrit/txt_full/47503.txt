Three reasons for this:
- Maloo is a dumb bot and it often picks the wrong LU ticket (or several wrong ones), and it needs a person to determine if the failure actually matches the ticket that was found
- our policy is that a test has to *pass* in order to land, not just "be a known intermittent failure", because otherwise it is possible that patches may land with "that failure can't possibly relate to my patch" but is actually *causing* the failure for whatever obscure reason (e.g. speeds up or slows down test/code, triggers a race elsewhere, etc.) and then lands and causes all patches to permanently  fail
- if every intermittent failure automatically "disappears" off the developer's radar, then there is no "friction" that makes them want to look at the failing test and fix it.  While annoying, if "unrelated" test failures are never looked at by developers, they will never be fixed, and code quality will only go down and never up.

I _try_ to look at these unrelated failures, moreso if they become frequent, and try to understand if this is a test issue, a code defect, or whatever, and then submit a patch if possible, or update the LU ticket with a +1 and try to pattern match vs. filesystem type, distro, try to isolate when the test started failing and attribute it to a particular patch, or whatever else can move it along a bit.
