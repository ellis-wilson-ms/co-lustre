LU-1158 general: support nanosec timestamps

Both server and client timestamps are nanosec by default, while client
timestamps are seconds for old servers.

Most changes are for backward compatibility:
* MDT/OST convert client times from second to nanosec for old clients
  in request unpack:
    . mdt_reint_unpack()
    . tgt_ost_body_unpack()
    . mdt/ofd_lvbo_fill() for glimpse
  and convert nanosec to second in reply pack for old clients:
    . tgt_pack_obdo()
    . tgt_pack_ost_lvb()
    . mdt_lvb2reply()
    . mdt_pack_attr2body()
* client doesn't need to handle times from/to requests, but it need to
  convert time to the granularity in use when handling times from user
  space or inode, and it also generates current time in proper
  granularity:
    . ts_to_cl_time() to convert timespec64 to client time
    . cl_time_to_sec_nsec() to convert time to sec and nanosec
    . cl_inode_get/set_xtime() to handle inode atime and etc.
    . cl_time_get() to get current time
* to support rolling upgrade, OSP/OUT may need to convert times in
  create/setattr/getattr.

Change lvb_a/m/ctime to paddings since they are not in use. And the
same for obdo.o_ioepoch because this field was unused since commit
v2_7_50_0-38-gd5d5b349f23e and was never used in production.

Update sanity.sh/test_39 series test cases to check nsec field in
timestamps.

CAVEATS: this may cause weird times in below rolling upgrade:
    1. upgrade client
    2. upgrade MDTs but not OSTs
    3. mount lustre on this client
    4. modify file times
