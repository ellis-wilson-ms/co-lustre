LU-14093 utils: fix format-overflow warning

Fix the following warning on gcc11 by making numbuf big enough to fit
format content.

lfs.c: In function ‘print_quota’:
lfs.c:7719:48: error: ‘sprintf’ may write a terminating nul past the end of the destination [-Werror=format-overflow=]
 7719 |                         sprintf(numbuf[0], "%s*", strbuf);
      |                                                ^
lfs.c:7719:25: note: ‘sprintf’ output between 2 and 33 bytes into a destination of size 32
 7719 |                         sprintf(numbuf[0], "%s*", strbuf);
      |                         ^~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Patch Set 1: Code-Review-1

> That is a real bug.

I'm not actually sure how it would be triggered. You'd need to have a quota limit that is at least 30 bytes long when printed, but a 2^64-byte number is only going to be 20 characters long, plus the [] and maybe * to mark over quota and a NUL makes 24 chars. 

It is probably unhappy because strbuf[32] could theoretically hold up to 31 chars+NUL (even though it could only really be 20+1), so adding the []* could push it over?

Probably the more correct solution is to declare strbuf[MAX_STRBUF-4] to quiet the compiler, and also use a bit less memory on the stack.
