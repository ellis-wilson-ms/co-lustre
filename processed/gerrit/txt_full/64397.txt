LU-19951 osc: fix kswapd writeback vs writer deadlock

When kswapd calls osc_flush_async_page(), it marks a page
PG_writeback via cl_page_prep() and sets oe_memalloc on the
extent, then adds it to the urgent list for ptlrpcd to
flush. However, if a writer thread calls osc_extent_find()
before ptlrpcd processes the extent, it can hold the extent
(transitioning it from OES_CACHE to OES_ACTIVE) and remove
it from the urgent list via list_del_init().

This creates a deadlock:
- The writer later hits the PG_writeback page in
  ll_write_begin() -> cl_page_assume() ->
  wait_on_page_writeback() and blocks.
- The extent is ACTIVE (held by the blocked writer), so
  ptlrpcd cannot flush it.
- The RPC is never submitted, so PG_writeback is never
  cleared.
- The writer waits forever.

Fix this by checking oe_memalloc in osc_extent_find()
before holding or merging an extent. When oe_memalloc is
set, the overlapping extent case treats it as a conflict
(waiting for the extent to be flushed), and the
non-overlapping case skips it. This ensures the extent
stays on the urgent list until ptlrpcd completes the RPC
and clears PG_writeback.
