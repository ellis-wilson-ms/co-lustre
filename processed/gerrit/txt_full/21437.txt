--- Message (Olaf Weber, 2016-08-09 12:13:41.000000000) ---
Patch Set 7: Code-Review-1

(1 comment)

It looks like the change goes through a lot of effort to copy the interface names from the lnet_ni before dropping the lnet_net_lock. But the lnet_net::ni_interfaces[] array is assigned when a ni is first initialized and immutable after that. The copying of the names is therefore not needed.

In LNetDist(), the lnet_net_lock protects against changes of the_lnet.ln_nis. In particular once the lnet_net_lock has been dropped the lnet_ni can be moved to the_lnet.ln_nis_zombie or even deallocated altogether. Taking a reference on the lnet_ni with lnet_ni_addref_locked() will protect against the latter but not the former.

Taking the_lnet.ln_api_mutex does protect against the_lnet.ln_nis being changed, because all DLC calls that can change the list use it.

On the other hand, if you're willing to assume that 'up' and 'addr' do not change for an lnet_ni after it has been created, then you could move this check to (for example) lnet_startup_lndni() and cache the result in the lnet_ni for use in LNetDist().
