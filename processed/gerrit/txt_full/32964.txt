--- Commit Message ---
LU-11227 lod: lod_sync: don't attempt sync to inactive targets

chgrp on a client triggers lod_sync() which in turn loops over OST/MDT
targets with dt_sync(). dt_sync() fails with -ENOTCONN when targets
have been deactivated (ie. set to active=0). The client retries
infinitely causing the client process to hang and considerably MDS
network traffic, load, and disk i/o.

the fix is to not attempt dt_sync() to ost/mdt targets that have been
deactivated and also (because of possible races) to ignore connection
errors.

tested with Lustre 2.10.4.

Hi Robin,
adding new Lustre tests is typically not too complex, often just a matter of finding some other existing test that does what you want to do, copy it, then change as needed.  It would be great if you could take a try at doing this, as we're always trying to get new people involved in Lustre development, and adding regression tests is something every developer should be able to do.

In this case, there are some tests for deactivated OSTs in lustre/tests/conf-sanity.sh test_50* that could be used as a starting point (test_50g() seems to be most suitable).  Just copy the test_50g() function, rename it to test_50j(), then change it to do the "touch $DIR/$tfile; chgrp $RUNAS_GID $DIR/$tfile" instead of "df -k", and if it doesn't hang then the test will be considered a pass.
