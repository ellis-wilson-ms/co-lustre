LU-12782 llite: rationalize cl_object_attr locking

The cl_object_attr lock protects the attributes of the
object and not anything else.  We seem to have cargo-culted
our way to taking the lock around writes to the thread
local cl_object_attr which is used to update the actual
object attributes.

This patch tries to clean that up and only take the lock
where it's actually needed.

We should test this patch for performance benefits and only
bother if the benefits are significant - they may be
trivial, and there are cases where we must take the lock to
read attributes that are non-obvious.  So there's a good
argument for leaving the current approach of also locking
around the attr prep, since it's simpler and the cost may
be insignificant.

But we should try to find out what the cost is.
