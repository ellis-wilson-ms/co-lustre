--- Commit Message ---
LU-1032 build: Support --disable-maintainer mode

We add "AM_MAINTAINER_MODE([enable])" to all configure
scripts to allow us to use --disable-maintainer-mode.

By default, without the AM_MAINTAINER_MODE macro, autotools
"maintainer mode" is enabled.  By specifying "enable" we
maintain our previous default behaviour.

Bruno, please be careful not to push new versions of patches like that. It can cause more work for the owner.

For example, in this instance, patch-set 1 passed Maloo but patch-set 2 didn't. The failure probably isn't due to this change, but now we must determine why it failed and then try and retest this patch until it passes. Unfortunately this can drag on the landing process unnecessarily.

If you need to base your work on a pending patch, I suggest you "checkout" the dependency and base your work on that. Rather than "cherry-picking" the pending patch onto a different branch. This will allow you to "git push" without affecting the other, unmodified, dependent patches.
