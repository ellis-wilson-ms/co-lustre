--- Commit Message ---
LU-168 Fix schedule race in sanityn PDO lock tests

In sanityn PDO lock tests, even if the second operation is blocked by
the first one on server-side, after the blocking, the second one may
be finished earlier than the first one because of client-side schedule
order. So sleep a sec before check_pdo_conflict() to ensure the first
operation is finished after OBD_FAIL_MDS_PDO_LOCK barriers.

--- Message (Fan Yong, 2011-07-06 01:58:41.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1:

> Adding these extra 1 second delay bits seems to be very time wasting thing, I wonder if there is any better/lightweight way to fix it?

In fact, for each PDO test, the fail_loc flag of "OBD_FAIL_MDS_PDO_LOCK" will block the OP1 at least 10s, and plus another 1s sleep before such blocking in the scripts, so increasing another 1s latency is not much time wasting.

On the other hand, the race between the two operations are mainly caused by client-side CPU schedule. It is not easy and unreasonable to make OP2 must finished after OP1, otherwise we cannot detect the real PDO test failure.
