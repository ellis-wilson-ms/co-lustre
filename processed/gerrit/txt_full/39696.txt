LU-13914 contrib: deprecate checkpatch.pl 80-column warning

Yes, staying withing 80 columns is certainly still _preferred_.  But
it's not the hard limit that the checkpatch warnings imply, and other
concerns can most certainly dominate.

Increase the default limit to 100 characters.  Not because 100
characters is some hard limit either, but that's certainly a "what are
you doing" kind of value and less likely to be about the occasional
slightly longer lines.

Miscellanea:

 - to avoid unnecessary whitespace changes in files, checkpatch will
   no longer emit a warning about line length when scanning files
   unless --strict is also used

 - Add a bit to coding-style about alignment to open parenthesis

Patch Set 1: Code-Review-1

Regardless of how upstream is going with this, I would prefer to keep 80-column wrapping for Lustre. As Arshad wrote, this allows terminal windows to be kept narrower, rather than having to make them wider just for a few long lines and have lots of dead space in the rightmost 20 columns because 99% of lines are already wrapped at 80 columns. 

That doesn't mean we cannot ever exceed 80 columns in the few cases they are needed, but I would prefer that those be the exceptions that are warned about, rather than having longer lines when they could still be wrapped easily.
