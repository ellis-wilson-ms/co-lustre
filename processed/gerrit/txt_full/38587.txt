--- Message (Patrick Farrell, 2023-02-24 01:04:06.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1:

Hmm.

I have mixed feelings about it.

It's a clever idea and the design looks sound.  Since we can combine non-adjacent IOs in to one RPC, it doesn't just rely on the application issuing poor AIOs (which was my initial objection).

But it *does* put us in the game of deciding when we should *delay* application I/O.  This isn't like caching writes, the AIOs can't complete until they're on disk, so when we make them wait... And then how long we do delay AIOs to wait for others to fit with them?  It's a game I don't really want to be playing, though it's one we could certainly win for some workloads.

So, I'd consider it if I saw a really good workload, but my instinct is no, just throw them out the door ASAP.
