As about Sebastien concern - it's completely wrong. rpm builds have an "auto requirement"  scripts who add an libselinux.so (or what is real library name) into requirements for package.
So library dependences completely don't needs and it's source of problems with building packages against of different rpm based distributions. (simple example SuSe don't have a selinux and use an apparmor).

These builds checked by 4 scripts (RHEL 7.6/8.1, SLES12/15) over ~10 configurations each. example of one of scripts is - https://pastebin.com/JdsrjxT7
