LU-14989 sec: keep encryption context in xattr cache

When an inode is being cleared, its xattr cache must be completely
wiped. But in case of lock cancel, we want to keep the encryption
context, as further processing might need to check it.

"lock cancel ... keep ..."
So, why is this safe :X

That's my question always when we retain something after lock cancellation.  Can this xattr be modified?  If so, how do we prevent it being stale and then conflicting on different nodes?  I would think the only thing safe to keep after lock cancellation would be something immutable.

When you say "further processing might need to check it" ... doesn't further processing just need to go get the lock again then?

--- Message (Sebastien Buisson, 2021-10-07 15:21:09.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1:

> "lock cancel ... keep ..."
 > So, why is this safe :X
 > 
 > That's my question always when we retain something after lock
 > cancellation.  Can this xattr be modified?  If so, how do we
 > prevent it being stale and then conflicting on different nodes?  I
 > would think the only thing safe to keep after lock cancellation
 > would be something immutable.

The encryption context is absolutely immutable for the whole lifetime of the file, from its initial creation to its removal from the file system. This is why it is safe to keep in cache after lock cancellation.

 > When you say "further processing might need to check it" ...
 > doesn't further processing just need to go get the lock again then?

I mean further processing of the inode still in client's memory, up to the close.

Does that make sense to you?
