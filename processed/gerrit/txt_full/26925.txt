Starting to look at lustre_rmmod,I'm wondering why the script as a whole can't go away. The script appears to do exactly what modprobe is meant to do. If you want to remove all lustre modules, that would simply be a 'modprobe -r libcfs' (since I think that's the lowest module in the stack). It would remove a maintenance burden as well.

Looking at the manpages online for Ubuntu, Suse, Fedora, and RHEL, it appears that 'modprobe -r' has been supported for a while.

This patch also lays down some ground work for other systemd services to be written to help ensure a clean shutdown.
