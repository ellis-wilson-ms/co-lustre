> The DEBUG messages are useful, so I'd like to keep them.

Are they?  How do you use them?
small GFP_NOFS allocations almost never fail.  They are much more likely to block than to fail.  I think the mostly fail when the processes has been killed by the OOM killer.

Being told that an allocation has succeeded doesn't seem particularly useful - the %p won't give you the address that was allocated any more, only a hash of that address.  You can still use that to correlate between messages I guess.  But if you see an address used, you know that it must have been allocated.
I guess the debug messages can tell you *when* a particular "me" was allocated.  Is that how they are useful?
Or did I miss something?
