--- Message (Patrick Farrell, 2017-03-07 17:05:37.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1:

We saw two issues with the grant code that prompted this.  One of them is clearly fixed by this, the other I'm less sure about.

1. Grant inflation caused a client request for just over 1 GiB of grant to become > 2 GiB and go past the per-client limit.  Resulted in 0 grant to client.
2. Clients using 4 MiB RPCs couldn't 'grow' their grant amount.  They send an RPC and then received 8 MiB of grant allocation, but (for some reason) couldn't use that to send two RPCs.  They just keep sending 1 RPC and staying at 8 MiB total grant.

Is it possible to get an explanation of how this fix relates to the second problem?

Patrick,

It does not directly address the second point.  I could not find a separate variation in logic for pre-GRANT_PARAM clients from these servers.  I can only say that ofd_grant_inflate() is used in a variety of places in addition to ofd_grant_chunk()

--- Message (Patrick Farrell, 2017-03-08 01:54:16.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1: Code-Review+1

Well, I just tested, and it fixes the second problem as well.

I note that with 16 MiB RPCs, we could still hit the 2 GiB limit on grant sizes.  (4096 pages per RPC * (256 + 1) rpcs in flight * 4096 bytes per page = 2^12*~2^8*2^12 = ~2^32 = ~4 GiB)

But I think that's an issue for another patch.
