LU-12838 ptlrpc: fix watchdog ratelimit logic

The ptlrpc-level watchdog ratelimiting is broken. The kernel prints:

    mdt00_009: service thread pid 18935 was inactive for 72s.
    Watchdog stack traces are limited to 3 per 300s, skipping...

even though there hasn't been any stack trace printed before.

It looks like the __ratelimit() return value is backward from
what one would expect from normal English grammar, namely that
if __ratelimit() returns true the action should NOT be limited.

Fix the logic checking the __ratelimit() return value, and add a
check in sanity test_422 (which forces a service thread timeout)
to ensure that the watchdog sometimes prints a full stack.

Fixes: fc9de679a4c2 ("LU-9859 libcfs: add watchdog for ptlrpc service threads")

--- Message (Andreas Dilger, 2019-10-09 00:26:55.000000000) ---
Patch Set 1:

> Was this discovered during recovery and the amount of debugging
 > keep increasing? I'm curious why just now it was found.

I was looking at the log of a patch test that failed due to LU-12749 (URL of log in LU-12838) that showed a watchdog timeout, but it didn't show the stack trace that I was interested in.  Looking back through the test log I saw a couple of other cases of "Watchdog stack traces are limited ..." being printed an hour earlier, but no case of a stack actually being printed by the watchdog.

I guess it was only noticed now because I was looking closely at the logs, and because we don't have a regression test that validates the correct behavior of this code.  I've definitely seen the "stack traces are limited" messages in recent test logs, but I wasn't looking for the stack trace at the time so figured it was normal due to lots of induced timeouts in the tests.  I didn't notice that the stack was *never* being printed, since most test regressions are pretty easy to figure out what the problem is without digging too deeply, but for new bugs that are hit in production that stack might be the only information that you get about why a server is hung.
